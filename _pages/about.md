I'm an epidemiologist working on what shapes our health as we age, and why that experience is so unequal. My research sits where population science meets health policy, on a simple conviction that good evidence should lead to better decisions.

I'm currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at NYU Langone's Optimal Aging Institute, studying hearing, cognition and healthy ageing through large cohort studies and randomised trials. I also support the European Commission's Joint Research Centre on the prevention of non-communicable diseases.

Over the past decade my work has followed three threads: the epidemiology of **tinnitus**, including the first global estimate of its prevalence, **tobacco control and prevention policy**, and **healthy ageing**. What ties them together is evidence synthesis, namely turning scattered data into something clinicians and policymakers can act on.
